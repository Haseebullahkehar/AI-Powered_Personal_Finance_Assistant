FINANCIAL LITERACY: KEY CONCEPTS EVERYONE SHOULD KNOW

1. COMPOUND INTEREST — THE EIGHTH WONDER OF THE WORLD
"Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it; he who doesn't, pays it." — often attributed to Einstein.

Simple interest: You earn interest only on your principal.
Compound interest: You earn interest on your principal AND previously earned interest.

Example:
- PKR 100,000 invested at 10% simple interest for 10 years = PKR 200,000
- PKR 100,000 invested at 10% compound interest for 10 years = PKR 259,374

The longer the time horizon, the more powerful compounding becomes.
Start investing early, even in small amounts.

2. INFLATION — THE SILENT THIEF
Inflation is the gradual rise in prices over time.
In Pakistan, inflation has averaged 10–20% in recent years.
Impact: PKR 10,000 today will only buy PKR 8,000–9,000 worth of goods next year.
Implication: Keeping all savings in cash means losing purchasing power.
Solution: Invest in assets that outpace inflation (stocks, real estate, gold, NSS).

3. NET WORTH — YOUR FINANCIAL SNAPSHOT
Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
Assets: cash, savings, investments, property, vehicle value
Liabilities: loans, credit card debt, mortgages

Track your net worth monthly. The goal is for it to grow over time.
A high income with high debt can result in low or negative net worth.

4. GOOD DEBT VS BAD DEBT
Good debt: Debt used to acquire appreciating assets or increase income potential.
- Education loan (increases earning potential)
- Business loan (generates revenue)
- Property mortgage (asset appreciates)

Bad debt: Debt used for depreciating assets or consumption.
- Credit card debt for shopping
- Personal loans for vacations
- Buy Now Pay Later for gadgets

Rule: If the return on the debt exceeds the interest rate, it may be good debt.

5. INSURANCE — PROTECTING WHAT YOU HAVE
Financial planning is not only about growing wealth — it's about protecting it.
Key insurance types:
- Health insurance: Covers medical emergencies. Essential.
- Life insurance: Provides for dependents if you pass away. Important if you have a family.
- Vehicle insurance: Legally required. Protects against accident costs.
- Home insurance: Covers fire, flood, theft.

In Pakistan: Jubilee, EFU, Adamjee, State Life are major providers.

6. UNDERSTANDING LOANS AND INTEREST RATES
Mark-up rate / profit rate (Islamic banking): The cost of borrowing.
APR (Annual Percentage Rate): Total yearly cost of a loan including fees.
Always compare APR, not just the monthly installment.

Tip: For car/home loans, even small extra payments dramatically reduce total interest paid.
Extra PKR 2,000/month on a PKR 1 million loan at 20% for 5 years saves significant interest.

7. INVESTMENT BASICS
Types of investments in Pakistan:
- Bank savings accounts: Low risk, low return
- National Savings Schemes (NSS): Government-backed, moderate return
- Mutual funds: Diversified, professionally managed
- Stocks (PSX): Higher risk, higher potential return
- Real estate: Illiquid but historically strong in Pakistan
- Gold: Hedge against inflation, highly liquid

Diversification: Don't put all eggs in one basket.
Risk and return are correlated — higher potential return = higher risk.

8. CREDIT SCORE AND CREDIT HISTORY (eCIB in Pakistan)
Pakistan's e-CIB (Credit Information Bureau) tracks your borrowing history.
A good credit history helps you:
- Get loans approved more easily
- Receive better interest rates
- Qualify for credit cards

Build good credit history:
- Pay all loan installments on time
- Never default
- Do not take on more debt than you can handle

9. FINANCIAL GOALS FRAMEWORK
Short-term (0–1 year): Emergency fund, vacation, gadget purchase
Medium-term (1–5 years): Car, education, wedding
Long-term (5+ years): Home, retirement, children's education

Match your investment vehicles to your time horizon.
Short-term goals: liquid, low-risk savings
Long-term goals: can tolerate higher risk for higher return

10. RETIREMENT PLANNING — START EARLY
Many Pakistanis rely on children or property in retirement.
Modern financial planning requires a dedicated retirement corpus.
EOBI (Employees Old-Age Benefits Institution) provides minimal support.
Supplement with:
- Voluntary Pension System (VPS)
- Mutual fund SIPs
- Real estate rental income
Rule of thumb: You need 20–25x your annual expenses at retirement.
If you spend PKR 1.2 million/year, you need PKR 24–30 million at retirement.
